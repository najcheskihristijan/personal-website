  • • Zero coworking spaces (first mover)

  • • Growing IT sector with established companies
  • • University population creates steady demand
  • • No alternative — people work from cafes or home

Risks to Monitor

Threats

  • ● Small addressable market in Prilep
  • ● Rent at 56% of opex (target 30-40%)
  • ● Declining population trend in the city
  • ● Seasonal student fluctuation (leave in summer)
  • ● Renovation investment on landlord’s property

  • ● Worst case is survivable (€428/partner)
  • ● Ratio improves as revenue grows (rent is fixed)
  • ● IT sector is growing against the trend
  • ● Backyard events + day passes fill summer gaps

Strategic Analysis

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • ● First mover — zero coworking spaces in Prilep
  • ● 300m²+ space at €3.70/m² — excellent value
  • ● No upfront rent — low cash barrier to launch
  • ● In-house marketing agency (EUR 0 marketing cost)
  • ● In-house tech team (EUR 0 website/booking system)
  • ● Two-floor separation creates natural premium tier
  • ● Backyard — unique amenity competitors can’t easily replicate
  • ● Worst-case monthly burn is survivable (€428/partner)
  • ● 3 partners = distributed risk and diverse skills
  • ● Minimal renovation needed — space is in good condition

Weaknesses

  • ● Rent is 56% of operating costs (industry ideal: 30-40%)
  • ● Break-even requires 18-19 members — nearly 60% occupancy
  • ● No proven demand — coworking is untested in Prilep
  • ● 3-partner decision making can slow execution
  • ● None of the partners run this full-time (assumption)
  • ● Renovation investment on landlord’s property
  • ● No track record in hospitality/space management
  • ● 300m² may be oversized for initial demand

Opportunities

  • ● IWConnect (200+ staff) could become anchor tenant
  • ● Growing remote work trend in Macedonia
  • ● 3,100+ university students — untapped pipeline
  • ● Event hosting revenue (backyard + ground floor)
  • ● Private office rental on upper floor for steady income
  • ● National “CoWork” brand expansion / franchising
  • ● Domain monopoly across 26 cities = future leverage
  • ● Government/EU grants for innovation spaces
  • ● Corporate partnerships (training venue, offsites)
  • ● Summer tourism angle — digital nomads passing through

Threats

  • ● Declining population in Prilep (long-term trend)
  • ● Economic downturn could reduce freelancer income
  • ● Landlord could refuse to renew lease after build-out
  • ● A competitor could open with more capital
  • ● Cafes adding “coworking corners” as a low-cost alternative
  • ● Seasonal student exodus in summer months
  • ● IWConnect or GrabIT building their own internal spaces
  • ● Energy price increases (HVAC is a major cost driver)
  • ● Partner disagreements or one partner exiting
